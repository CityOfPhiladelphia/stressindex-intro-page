Stress Index

By combining key measures of poverty, health, education, employment, and quality of life, the Stress Index show which neighborhoods experience different kinds of stressors.  These maps are used to understand the unique context of each neighborhood, so that the city can better meet the needs of residents.

 

How you can use the Stress Index:

  • Find the percentage of people living in poverty in a particular neighborhood.
  • See what parts of the city experience the highest temperatures.
  • Identify areas of high and low drug crime arrests.
  • Examine unemployment levels across different parts of the city.
  • See median household income levels in different neighborhoods.
